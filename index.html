Why is water tank cleaning important?

Over 60% of illnesses are waterborne, making it the leading cause of illness among children. Children under the age of 5 are especially susceptible to diseases caused by contaminated water. One of the most common ways they come into contact with contaminated water is through water storage tanks. These tanks, if not regularly cleaned or cleaned improperly by local plumbers or sweepers, can harbor harmful pathogens and significantly contribute to the spread of illness. Proper maintenance and cleaning of water storage tanks by trained professionals are essential to prevent waterborne diseases.

FAQ’s (Frequently Asked Questions)



How often should I schedule water tank cleaning services?

It is recommended to schedule water tank cleaning services twice a year to prevent sediment build-up that can contaminate the water. If you notice any foreign particles, suspect the presence of germs, or find any dead animals in the tank, you should immediately seek professional cleaning services.

How long does the water tank cleaning process take?

The duration of the cleaning process varies based on the size of the tank. For a 1,000-liter tank, it typically takes around 30-45 minutes. Our professional team will provide you with an estimated time frame and inform you of any potential delays.

How frequently should my water tank be cleaned?

The frequency of cleaning depends on the tank's condition and age. The Ministry of Health recommends cleaning water tanks every six months to prevent contamination and ensure the water is safe for drinking, cooking, bathing, and washing. Older tanks may require more frequent cleaning compared to newer ones.

How long does the entire cleaning process take?

The time required for the cleaning process depends on whether the tank is already drained. If the water is pre-drained, the process can be completed within a few hours.

What sizes of tanks do you clean?

We clean water tanks with capacities ranging from 1,000 liters to 200,000 liters.

What are the steps involved in the tank cleaning process?

Our comprehensive cleaning process is conducted by professionals to ensure your tanks are thoroughly cleaned and hygienic. The service includes the following steps:

1. De-watering the tank using mechanized methods
2. Removing the sludge
3. Vacuuming the tank
4. Performing UV treatment
5. Cleaning with a water jet process and surface cleaning
6. Applying an anti-bacterial spray

Do you use chemicals during the cleaning process?

No, we prioritize safety and use only organic products that meet safety standards for you and your surroundings.

What happens to the dirt removed from the tank?

The tanks are cleaned with an organic disinfectant, and the water is vacuumed using an industrial water vacuum hover.
